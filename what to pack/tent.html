Top 10 Things To Carry For Camping

Essential camping accessories


So if you are preparing for a camping tour, here are 10 camping accessories you must have in your backpack.

Tents

Whether you’re going out on a long or short trip that will have you and someone else sleeping outdoors overnight, a 2-person tent is a must-have.

Sleeping Bags

Going without a sleeping bag risks an uncomfortable night's sleep at best, exposure at worst.

Water Bottles

Filtered water bottles are designed to filter chemical impurities from municipal sources, while others can handle microbiologically unsafe water from any global source.

First Aid Kit

Your first aid kit should include other necessities as well: scissors, adhesive, gauze, soap, a CPR mouth barrier and an emergency whistle. Be sure to toss in bottles of sunscreen .

Pocket Knife

The pocket knife is the ultimate multipurpose tool of the outdoors.If you leave your knife at home, expect to feel frustrated–often.

Maps

Constant changes in the sun's position can make forest landmarks seem unfamiliar, causing hikers to become disoriented. Hence, maps are neccesary tool.

Toilet Papers

If you're camping in the deep woods and concerned about the environmental impact of using toilet paper, purchase some that's biodegradable or take a refuse bag for disposal.

Torch

. If you need to locate an item within your tent or visit the latrine during the night, a portable, battery-powered light is invaluable.

Apporopriate Clothing

Camping means having only a few changes of clothes available, so it's important to keep them dry. Choose a lightweight, waterproof rain jacket that will accommodate multiple layers of clothing.

Mosquito repellent

Mosquito bites are irritating and can even lead you to getting some diseases. Hence, never forget to toss some Mosquito repellent in your bag.
